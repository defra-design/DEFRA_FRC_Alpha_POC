Report your forest risk commodity due diligence

If you use forest risk commodities as part of your business activity in the UK, you may be required to undertake a due diligence exercise on your supply chains, and to report on this exercise annually to show that those commodities were grown on or sourced from land legally occupied or owned.

Businesses within the scope of this legislation can use this service to:

  • File and amend exemption
  • Submit reports detailing evidence of due diligence activity
Find out who needs to report their forest risk commodity due diligence.

If your business meets the following requirements, you will be required to complete reporting activities:

  • Trades at or above the threshold for annual turnover
  • Uses the specified forest risk commodities within its UK commercial activity at or above the specified volumes

If your business is a subsidiary of another business, for example a holding company, that meets the annual turnover requirements, you may need to provide financial information about your parent company.

If your business uses levels of relevant commodities that are below the specified usage thresholds, you may be eligible to file for an exemption from this reporting requirement.

This process will take less than 15 minutes.

Before you start

To complete this process you will:

  • Provide the annual turnover of your business,and your parent company if applicable
  • Choose the forest risk commodities that your businessuses in any UK commercial activitity
  • Provide the volume of usage for those commodities
  • Describe or supply the methods you used to determine your usage
  • Provide evidence of your due diligence activities

To do this, before you begin make sure that you have:

  • Reviewed the guidance about when to report and what information you will need to provide
  • Collected the evidence required to submit your exemption or annual report
